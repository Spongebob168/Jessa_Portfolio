Flexible Learning Design

Adapting instruction to meet every learner's needs.

🔄

What is Flexible Learning?

Flexible learning is an educational approach that provides students with choices in how, when, and where they learn. It acknowledges that learners have different needs, backgrounds, and circumstances. In the Philippine context, it includes modular distance learning, online learning, blended learning, and face-to-face instruction adapted for diverse settings.

📚

Flexible Learning in PE

Physical Education can be delivered flexibly through home-based exercises, video-guided activity modules, virtual fitness challenges, and asynchronous movement tasks. Teachers design activities that students can perform safely at home with minimal equipment, ensuring inclusive participation regardless of physical resources.

🧩

Differentiated Instruction

Differentiated instruction means adjusting content, process, and assessment to match individual student needs. In PE, this may involve offering modified activities for students with physical limitations, providing visual/audio instructions for different learning styles, or allowing students to progress at their own pace within a unit.

🌱

Benefits of Flexible Design

Flexible learning design promotes equity, inclusion, and student agency. It reduces learning gaps caused by absences or resource limitations, supports learners in diverse communities, and empowers students to take ownership of their health and fitness journey even outside school hours.

✏️

Reflection Prompt

How would you design a flexible PE lesson that students could complete both at school and at home?
